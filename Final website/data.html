Data


Reasons of not using contraceptive services

Description

The data is collected from 503 girls and 623 boys, all of them are secondary students. We find out that 59% of the girls prefer not to use contraceptive services because it encourages pre-marital sex. And the second reason is that the services encourage promiscuity. And their reasons are same as those of boys.

Gaps between actual and expected sexual education

Description

We conducted a survey about the differences between actual and expected sexual education. And 41 university students responded to our survey. We found out that school put more attention on teaching human development, such as puberty, body image and reporduction, and sexual behaviours. However, most of them would prefer to learn the culture, human rights and society. Apart from learning sexual behaviour, they would also like to learn social values, attitudes and skills.

*P.s. The total number of "What university students have learnt" and "What university students want to learn" are different because some of them may have no opinions on the second questions.

Legal ages of having sex

Description

The data showed that all four groups prefer to have the legalised sexual intercourse at 18. And we found out that the second highest options for girls (secondary school) and females (aged 18-27) are the same - at 20 or above. However, both second highest options for boys (secondary school) and males (aged 18-27) are at 16. And there are 5% of boys prefer to have legalised sexual intercourse at 11 or below. It showed that boys/males prefer to have legalised sexual intercourse earlier than girls/females.

Mean ages of having the first sexual intercourse

Description

The mean ages of having the first sexual intercourse of the four groups are between 14 and 19 years old from 1996 to 2016. It showed that the mean ages remains low in this 10 years.
