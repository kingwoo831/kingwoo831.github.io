Background

Sexual Education in Hong Kong is available starts in primary school. However, the guideline on sexual education has been criticised for being out of date. According to the Hong Kong government, the guideline on sexual education is last updated in 1997. The key concept covered five elements such as human development, health and behaviour, interpersonal relationships; and marriage and family. The effectiveness of sexual education has been doubted by the government as there is a surge in HIV infection from 181 in 1997 to 692 in 2016, according to the Department of Health. At the same time, there is a surge in young pregnancy and young pregnancy of unmarried couples from 2011 - 2016 amongst 18 to 27 years old, according to the family association of Hong Kong. Due to these two phenomenons of the ineffectiveness of sexual education and the rise of young pregnancy, our study raised the question of whether there is a direct link between them.
